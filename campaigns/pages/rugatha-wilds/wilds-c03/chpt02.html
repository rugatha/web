Chapter 2: Banditry is a State of Mind

Storm of Mudtown

15049.03.23 – 15049.03.25

15049.03.23

距離康妮與冒險者們說好的午夜還有很長的時間，冒險者們思索著該如何妥善利用。在阿龍的帶領下，大家先到了城內的雜貨店，進行補給。阿龍買了一些釘子，以預備上山後和山賊們的戰鬥。其他冒險者們也進行了簡單的補給。

雜貨店內沒有阿龍想買的藥水，因此大家就到了隔壁的藥品店。然而因為藥水的要價不菲，大家決定作罷。毛毛的神色卻有些不自然，似乎是趁著老闆不注意時，幹了什麼勾當。

離開藥品店後，冒險者們決定分頭進行。雷亞看見了阿龍買的釘子，腦中冒出了些點子，因此動身前往附近的樹林，找尋他眼中合適的樹枝；鑰和毛毛先返回渡鴉中樞見見阿龍的父母，順便簡單休息；羅羅去了趟武器行，買了一把輕弩和一組箭。不久後，雷亞將樹枝帶回，和阿龍要了釘子後，便簡單的手工做了一把釘頭錘，雖然沒有像武器行那麼穩固，不過還算堪用。

接近傍晚，冒險者們都已回到了他們在渡鴉中樞三樓的客房。大家各自取了晚餐，阿龍也特別為綠先生準備了一份。接著大家便一起走到了二樓的萊里和綠先生的辦公室。

此時冒險者們只見綠先生一人獨自在辦公室內。阿龍將晚餐遞給綠先生以釋出善意，同時大家也和綠先生詢問著稍晚上山「剿匪」的計劃。他們這才發現，綠先生從來沒有跟隨康妮或春天精靈上山過。起初，綠先生對於上山這一件事，也沒有多大的興趣，但在冒險者們的請求下，他才勉為其難的答應。同時，萊里正好回到辦公室，也答應了讓綠先生一起上山的計劃。

不過，由於冒險者們需要在午夜時和康妮等人集合，雖然此時才剛日落左右，也得先休息了，以免精神不濟。

門口傳來叩叩叩的敲門聲。萊里在完成公務後，一一朝冒險者們的房間敲門，提醒他們起床準備。

冒險者們帶著與山賊奮戰的心，來到二樓與綠先生會合。大家一起走向與康妮說好的集合地點—議會門口。然而，此時冒險者們看到的就只有提前抵達，正在玩鬧的「壞四人」。

阿龍試著和壞四人溝通，要他們安靜點，不要吵到其他居民，但他很快地發現壞四人和他離開村莊前相比，沒有什麼長進，根本難以溝通。甚至稍早他手動洗過的皮柏身上的味道早已恢復成原本的狀態了。

不久後，遠方傳來兩人的腳步聲。毛毛雨下，康妮撐著傘，傘下的佩妮踏著輕快的步伐，高聲朝著大家打招呼。雖然面對佩妮的情緒已變得有些扭曲，阿龍還是挺開心的。

康妮看見初次加入隊伍的綠先生，有些困惑，但在佩妮認為沒有妨礙的前提下，也就讓綠先生同行了。

15049.03.24

午夜，春天精靈與他的春天騎士團們出發，前往海雅特山，準備去見名為「海雅特山交響樂」的山賊。

路程中，壞四人聒噪又忍人厭的各種胡鬧，讓阿龍看了很不開心，內心也質問著「到底佩妮為什麼會把這些人要召進他的春天騎士團內？」但當然，他也沒有得到答案。

早餐與午餐都在路邊簡單用過後，日落前，春天騎士團終於來到了海雅特山交響樂的領地口。康妮提醒大家，要大家不要招惹山賊，如果他們不讓大家進去，也不要強迫闖入。這讓大家也感到有些困惑—既然大家是要去與山賊交戰，還談什麼禮節呢？

領地口兩根高聳的樹幹前，各站了一名山賊，而其中一名很顯然的就是大奧斯了，佩妮一見到他，彷彿身體沒有任何不適，快速地衝了上去抱緊他。看在阿龍眼裡，格外眼紅。

佩妮請求大奧斯讓他的春天騎士團一起進入領地內，而大奧斯看了看所有來訪的人們，最終決定，除了「議會的走狗」康妮外，其他人都可以進去。對於第一次來就能輕鬆進去的綠先生，康妮很不是滋味，他試圖與山賊們溝通，卻只迎來大奧斯呼喚一名女性山賊同胞將康妮接到領地口外的帳篷休息區。

隨著康妮消失在視野外，大奧斯和他的夥伴像是鬆了一口氣般，卸下了嚴肅的神情，嬉鬧著、笑著，迎接春天騎士團們一起走入領地。他招呼著大家，關心著大家是否肚子餓了還沒吃晚餐？接著帶著大家朝著領地中心走去。大家詫異著，這群「山賊」竟然毫無惡意，還如此友善，與土城內大家描述的「收取過路費的惡霸」完全不一樣。大奧斯也笑著說，這就是為何不讓康妮進來的原因—不能讓議會知道真相。冒險者們似懂非懂，但對於「討伐山賊」這件事，也更沒有把握了。

一路上，大奧斯快速地向他們介紹，他們有自己的農場、牧場、餐廳、酒館，可以說是自給自足，比起山賊的營地，更像是個小型的聚落。來到中央的廣場，幾名山賊正用木柴搭建起一座營火，大奧斯示意大家先坐下來休息，他也和佩妮找了個角落坐了下來，看得阿龍又眼紅了。

一名山賊彈奏著魯特琴，一名則身著類似涅西斯風格的教袍，像個牧師一樣，還有一名山賊遞了酒給大家喝。冒險者們與山賊們漸漸開啟了溝通。雷亞和牧師習得了許多他不知道的關於涅西斯的教義；鑰也和當地的「財務官」山賊拿到了他們詳盡的收支記錄，發現從土城徵收的錢，除了部分繳交給他們的上級組織外，其他幾乎都是拿來買作物或和附近的聚落打交道用的。

除此之外，還有一名看起來有點笨拙的新手打鐵匠山賊，十分討冒險者們歡心。

在冒險者們的詢問下，他們得知海雅特山交響樂的上級組織，是自稱「砍殺交響樂」的組織—他們視殺戮為一種美妙的音樂，享受其中。而砍殺交響樂的組織龐大，除了如海雅特山交響樂這種聚於山上的「山賊」外，各大城市內的黑道、黑市等也幾乎都是他們的下線，影響力擴及整個帝國。介於砍殺交響樂與土城之間的海雅特山交響樂則身份尷尬，他們對土城的惡形，幾乎都是基於要交代給砍殺交響樂的目的，要是逃跑，只會被取下人頭拎回來示眾。對此，冒險者們聽了，提供了大奧斯一些建議，但對大奧斯來說都不夠好。

在佩妮的同意下，大奧斯也提出了佩妮過去的提議：他曾私下和大奧斯提出私奔的想法，兩人一起逃離這是非之地，換個身份，浪漫的活下去。佩妮認為家人什麽都他都能拋棄，但對於大奧斯的愛，他放不下。而大奧斯也因為放不下對他的手下，他的兄弟們的義氣，而無法承諾私奔。

吃飽喝足後，在牧師的引領下，佩妮踏出營火圈，走向營火邊的牧師。儀式就要開始了。

在牧師帶頭吟誦後，佩妮身上的翠綠印記開始發光，而佩妮則面露難色，但不久後，佩妮的神色開始緩解。冒險者們仔細一看，發現營火圈外的山賊們此時卻直冒冷汗，咬牙切齒—就像是佩妮身上的痛處，轉移到他們身上去了一樣。這讓冒險者們更困惑了。

儀式結束後，佩妮很明顯的看起來舒服與開心很多。他大聲謝謝牧師與其他山賊後，便快步回到大奧斯身邊。對此，冒險者們有很多疑惑，便向牧師詢問。牧師表示這個儀式是古老的 Nessis 儀式，用處是分攤痛處，而把他用在春天精靈身上，只是他們的主意罷了，說白了，和砍殺交響樂也沒有什麼關係。佩妮看向冒險者，表示自己說每個月上山很辛苦的部分，其實是爬山的時候，他很感激山賊們為他分攤痛楚。

冒險者們繼續詢問，得知砍殺交響樂會不定期上山來催款，如果沒有繳出應有的款項，就會有人頭落地—就算有繳出，也不保證所有人都能安全活下來。也因此，海雅特山交響樂幾乎每個月都在徵招新人，那名呆呆的新手打鐵匠，就是上個月剛加入的新人。

阿龍的內心很混亂：面對如此難以預測又霸道的上級組織，海雅特山交響樂卻也不逃脫、不反抗；說好上山要剿匪，結果這群山賊竟然是一群請大家喝酒吃肉、唱歌跳舞的山民；還有，佩妮的手什麼時候才要從大奧斯身上拿下來，不對，他的嘴唇怎麼貼上大奧斯的臉頰了！？

山賊們向冒險者們介紹了提供給他們過夜的空間：一個人一間獨立的小木屋，讓大家不受打擾。（鑰決定先把毛毛安置在和他相同的房間，以免入夜後阿龍跑去刮毛毛的毛，或是像之前一樣把毛毛當成毛巾來用—而毛毛在營火前，似乎已經從眾山賊們口袋中摸走了不少錢。）

大奧斯也告訴大家，明天早上在大家下山前，可以讓大家參觀一下他們這裡的各個環境。

冒險者們睡前簡單討論了一下明天的計劃：「剿匪」這條路似乎是行不通了，甚至可以說，上山這趟根本就是白費了。要拿到蘋果木，最簡單的做法，似乎還是下山後偷偷潛入 Applewood 家的領地，砍斷蘋果木，讓它從維爾河順流而下，達成萊里要的條件。

15049.03.25

醒來後，冒險者們簡單收拾，來到已熄滅的營火前，和山賊拿了早餐。

雷亞在一名曾任警官的山賊的帶領下，到附近的樹林深處，感受 Nessis 的力量。他發現，在這裡，是近期他所能感受最強烈的神性，甚至沒有任何殺戮的痕跡，讓他感到非常平靜。

冒險者們、綠先生與壞四人等在大奧斯的帶領下，走到了領地口，此時康妮也在負責他起居的山賊帶領下，與他們會面。佩妮依依不捨地和大奧斯道別，眾人準備下山。

「—他、他們來了！」一聲喧囂打破佩妮和大奧斯的離別之情，也敲醒了阿龍逐漸消去的戰意。

15049.03.23

There was still a long time to go before the midnight meeting Connie had arranged with the adventurers, so they considered how best to make use of it. Led by Al-lon, they first went to a general store in town to resupply. Al-lon bought some nails to prepare for the battle with the bandits after they headed up the mountain. The other adventurers also did some simple restocking.

The general store did not have the potions Al-lon wanted, so the group went next door to the apothecary. However, since the potions were quite expensive, everyone decided against buying any. Fur, however, looked slightly unnatural, as if he had gotten up to something while the shopkeeper was not paying attention.

After leaving the apothecary, the adventurers split up. Leah saw the nails Al-lon had bought and got a few ideas, so she headed to the nearby woods to look for branches she considered suitable. Kaname and Fur returned first to the Raven Nexus to see Al-lon's parents and get a little rest. Roel went to a weapons shop and bought a light crossbow and a set of bolts. Not long after, Leah returned with some branches, asked Al-lon for the nails, and quickly crafted a crude nail-headed mace by hand. It was not as sturdy as anything from a weapons shop, but it was usable enough.

By early evening, all the adventurers had returned to their guest rooms on the third floor of the Raven Nexus. Everyone picked up dinner for themselves, and Al-lon also specially prepared a portion for Mr. Green. Then they all went together to Riley and Mr. Green's office on the second floor.

At that moment, they found only Mr. Green alone in the office. Al-lon handed him the dinner as a gesture of goodwill, while the others asked him about the plan to go up the mountain later and "wipe out the bandits." They only then discovered that Mr. Green had never accompanied Connie or the Spring Spirit up the mountain before. At first, he had little interest in going, but after the adventurers pressed him, he reluctantly agreed. Just then Riley returned to the office as well, and also approved the plan for Mr. Green to go along.

However, since the adventurers needed to meet Connie and the others at midnight, even though the sun had only just set, they still needed to rest first so they would not be exhausted.

A knocking sound came from the door. After finishing her work, Riley went around knocking on each adventurer's room to remind them to get up and prepare.

With their hearts set on battling bandits, the adventurers met up with Mr. Green on the second floor. Together they headed to the agreed meeting point with Connie, the entrance to the council hall. However, the only people there when they arrived were Evil Be-4, who had shown up early and were fooling around.

Al-lon tried to talk to Evil Be-4 and tell them to quiet down so they would not disturb the other residents, but he quickly realized that compared to before he left the village, they had not improved at all and were impossible to reason with. Even Piper, whom he had personally scrubbed earlier, already smelled just as bad again as before.

Not long after, the sound of two approaching sets of footsteps came from the distance. In a light drizzle, Connie walked under an umbrella, while Penny beneath it stepped forward briskly and greeted everyone loudly. Even though Al-lon's feelings toward Penny had become somewhat twisted by now, he was still happy to see her.

Connie looked a little confused when she noticed Mr. Green joining the group for the first time, but since Penny said it would not cause any trouble, she let him come along.

15049.03.24

At midnight, the Spring Spirit and her Spring Knights set out for Mount Hyatt to meet the bandits known as the Mount Hyatt Symphony.

Along the way, Evil Be-4 carried on with their loud, irritating antics, which made Al-lon deeply unhappy. Inwardly he kept asking himself, "Why on earth would Penny recruit people like this into her Spring Knights?" Naturally, he got no answer.

After eating a simple breakfast and lunch by the roadside, the Spring Knights finally reached the entrance to the Mount Hyatt Symphony's territory before sunset. Connie reminded everyone not to provoke the bandits, and said that if they were refused entry, they should not force their way in. This left everyone somewhat confused. If they were supposedly here to fight bandits, why were they worrying about manners?

At the entrance, two bandits stood before a pair of towering tree trunks, and one of them was very obviously Big Os. The moment Penny saw him, she rushed over and threw her arms around him as if her body felt no discomfort at all. To Al-lon, the sight was especially painful.

Penny asked Big Os to let her Spring Knights enter the territory with her. Big Os looked over everyone who had come and eventually decided that everyone except Connie, "the council's lapdog," could enter. Connie was clearly displeased that Mr. Green, who had never come before, was being allowed inside so easily. She tried to argue with the bandits, but all that got her was Big Os calling over a female bandit companion to escort Connie to a tented rest area outside the entrance.

As soon as Connie disappeared from view, Big Os and his companions seemed to breathe a sigh of relief. Their stern expressions dropped away, and they laughed and joked as they welcomed the Spring Knights inside. He greeted everyone warmly, asking whether they were hungry and had not yet eaten dinner, then led them toward the center of the territory. Everyone was stunned. These "bandits" were not hostile at all, and were in fact so friendly that they were nothing like the toll-collecting brutes the people of Mudcastle had described. Big Os laughingly said that this was exactly why Connie was not allowed in, the council could not be permitted to learn the truth. The adventurers only half understood, but they felt even less certain than before about this supposed "bandit extermination."

As they walked, Big Os quickly introduced the place to them. They had their own farms, ranches, restaurant, and tavern. It was basically self-sufficient, less like a bandit camp and more like a small settlement. When they reached the central square, several bandits were building a bonfire out of firewood. Big Os gestured for everyone to sit down and rest, and he and Penny found a corner to sit together in, much to Al-lon's jealousy.

One bandit played a lute, another wore robes in a style similar to that of Nessis and looked almost like a priest, and yet another passed around drinks for everyone. Gradually, the adventurers and the bandits began talking. Leah and the priest exchanged a great deal of doctrine about Nessis that Leah had never known before. Kaname also obtained the group's detailed records of income and expenditures from the local bandit "treasurer," and discovered that apart from the portion sent up to their superiors, almost all of the money collected from Mudcastle was used to buy crops or trade with nearby settlements.

There was also a somewhat clumsy novice blacksmith among the bandits, and the adventurers found him extremely endearing.

In response to the adventurers' questions, they learned that the Mount Hyatt Symphony answered to an upper organization calling itself the Slaughter Symphony, a group that viewed killing as a beautiful kind of music and took pleasure in it. The Slaughter Symphony was vast. In addition to mountain "bandit" groups like the Mount Hyatt Symphony, the underworld, black markets, and similar organizations in major cities were almost all subordinate to them, and their influence spread across the entire empire. The Mount Hyatt Symphony, stuck awkwardly between the Slaughter Symphony and Mudcastle, was in a difficult position. Their cruelty toward Mudcastle was almost entirely for the sake of having something to report upward to the Slaughter Symphony. If they tried to run, their heads would simply be chopped off and brought back as a warning. The adventurers listened and offered Big Os some suggestions, but none of them were good enough from his point of view.

With Penny's consent, Big Os also brought up a proposal Penny had made in the past. She had privately suggested that the two of them elope, escape this tangled place together, take on new identities, and live a romantic life elsewhere. Penny believed she could throw away everything else, even family, but she could not let go of her love for Big Os. And Big Os, unable to abandon his bond with his subordinates, his brothers, could not promise to run away with her.

After everyone had eaten and drunk their fill, Penny stepped out from the ring of the bonfire and walked toward the priest standing beside it. The ritual was about to begin.

Once the priest began the chant, the Verdant Seal on Penny's body started to glow. Penny grimaced in pain, but before long her expression began to ease. Looking more closely, the adventurers realized that the bandits standing outside the bonfire circle were instead sweating heavily and clenching their teeth, as if Penny's pain had somehow been transferred onto them. This only left the adventurers more confused.

When the ritual ended, Penny very clearly looked far more comfortable and cheerful. After loudly thanking the priest and the other bandits, she hurried back to Big Os's side. The adventurers had many questions, so they asked the priest. He explained that this ritual was an ancient Nessis rite used to divide and share pain, and that using it on the Spring Spirit was simply their own idea. To put it bluntly, it had nothing to do with the Slaughter Symphony at all. Penny turned to the adventurers and said that when she had complained about how hard it was to come up the mountain each month, what she had really meant was the climb itself, and that she was grateful to the bandits for sharing her pain.

The adventurers kept asking questions and learned that the Slashing Symphony came up the mountain at irregular times to collect dues. If the proper payments were not made, heads would roll, and even if payment was made, there was no guarantee that everyone would survive safely. Because of that, the Mount Hyatt Symphony recruited new members almost every month. That clumsy novice blacksmith was one such newcomer, having joined just last month.

Al-lon's heart was in utter chaos. Faced with such an overbearing and unpredictable higher organization, the Mount Hyatt Symphony neither fled nor resisted. They had come up the mountain to kill bandits, yet these bandits were mountain folk who invited everyone to drink, eat meat, sing, and dance. And another thing, when exactly was Penny going to take her hand off Big Os? No, wait, when did her lips get pressed against his cheek?!

The bandits showed the adventurers the sleeping arrangements they had prepared: one private cabin per person, so everyone could rest undisturbed. Kaname decided to keep Fur in the same room as her for the time being, lest Al-lon decide in the middle of the night to shave Fur's fur again, or use Fur like a towel as before. Fur, meanwhile, seemed to have already lifted quite a bit of money from the pockets of the gathered bandits while they were around the bonfire.

Big Os also told everyone that the next morning, before they went back down the mountain, he could show them around the various parts of the settlement.

Before going to sleep, the adventurers briefly discussed their plan for tomorrow. The route of "bandit extermination" seemed completely unworkable. In fact, it almost felt as though this whole trip up the mountain had been pointless. If they wanted to obtain the Applewood branch, the simplest method now seemed to be sneaking into the Applewood family's land after going back down, cutting off an apple branch, and letting it float down the Veil River to fulfill Riley's condition.

15049.03.25

After waking up, the adventurers tidied themselves up and came to the extinguished bonfire, where the bandits handed them breakfast.

Led by a bandit who had once served as a police officer, Leah went deep into the nearby woods to feel the power of Nessis. She found that here, more than anywhere she had been recently, she could sense the strongest divine presence, and there was not even a trace of killing in it, which left her feeling deeply peaceful.

The adventurers, Mr. Green, and Evil Be-4 followed Big Os to the edge of the territory, where Connie was waiting for them as well, having been brought there by the bandit assigned to care for her. Penny reluctantly said goodbye to Big Os, and the group prepared to head back down the mountain.

"T-they're here!" A sudden shout shattered the tender mood of parting between Penny and Big Os, and snapped Al-lon's fading fighting spirit awake once more.
