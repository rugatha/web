A rapier thrust toward the back of a Mount Hyatt Symphony bandit who had just run back from the distance, but Al-lon knocked the blade aside in time and saved the bandit's life.

Three figures in black leather armor, the human man who had been holding the rapier, a large goblin grinning broadly, and a burly gray-skinned tiefling, reined in their horses and slowly dismounted. It was the first time Al-lon had ever seen Big Os's eyes filled with fear. The goblin dragged a burlap sack in each hand.

Under the tiefling's intimidation, Big Os impulsively declared that this time they would not be paying the higher-ups any more money, and that they would resist to the bitter end. Riley asked Mr. Green to drive the carriage and take the adventurers to Rathanad, while also picking up the analysis results that had been commissioned in the neighboring town on the return trip. Mr. Green felt helpless. Not only had he risked life and limb alongside the adventurers and still not gotten any gold, but the very next day after coming back down the mountain, he would have to leave again on another business trip. Still, as Riley's employee, he had no choice.

With Al-lon's permission, Leah borrowed the green dragon horn Al-lon had acquired and decided to turn it into a necklace so Al-lon could wear it around his neck.

Maribel, meanwhile, made up her mind to leave the rotten little town of Mudtown behind. Accompanied by Kaname and Fur, they walked back to town with her so she could pack her things. She intended to follow her father's final words and step out into the wider world.

As they entered Mudtown, something in a tree to the side caught Maribel's eye, though she quickly turned away again. Kaname and Fur only noticed that something seemed off about her, and when their questions got no answer, they let the matter drop.
